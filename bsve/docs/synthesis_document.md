# BSVE Synthesis 01

## Persistence as a Candidate Latent Behavioral Variable

Research status: Exploratory synthesis

Source studies:

- Reactive-JPY outcome discovery
- Reactive-CHF volatility-conditioned persistence studies
- Historical Persistent vs Reactive family investigations

------

# Purpose

The purpose of this document is to identify behavioral mechanisms that appear to generalize across multiple pair families and ontological frameworks.

Reactive-JPY and Reactive-CHF were originally investigated as separate research programs. However, both studies ultimately converged on persistence-related phenomena despite beginning from different hypotheses and using different ontology structures.

This document summarizes those findings and evaluates whether persistence may represent a deeper latent behavioral variable within BSVE.

The objective is not to define a new ontology.

The objective is to determine whether multiple ontology families may be describing different manifestations of the same underlying behavioral process.

------

# Historical Context

The original Persistent and Reactive family labels did not emerge from persistence-magnitude ranking or duration statistics.

Instead, the partition emerged from:

- grouped deep-learning training experiments,
- cross-family transfer studies,
- ABM calibration asymmetries,
- downstream MPML integration behavior.

The family labels therefore describe observed structural differences in sentiment-driven behavior rather than any single measured variable.

Importantly, neither family was originally defined in terms of persistence duration.

The CHF studies therefore represent the first systematic attempt to examine persistence itself as a candidate explanatory mechanism.

------

# Reactive-JPY Findings

## Original Ontology

Reactive-JPY models the lifecycle of extreme consensus episodes.

States:

- Young
- Maturing
- Mature

The ontology was calibrated using empirical reversal-hazard and survival analysis.

The resulting state machine describes how long extreme crowd-consensus states survive before reversal.

------

## Outcome Discovery

The strongest outcome-family discovered in Reactive-JPY was crowd-failure probability.

Key finding:

Maturing episodes exhibit substantially higher crowd-failure rates than Young episodes.

24-bar horizon:

- Young failure rate: 54.2%
- Maturing failure rate: 70.7%

The effect:

- survives pair decomposition,
- survives horizon variation,
- survives transformation to crowd-relative outcomes,
- exhibits exploratory statistical support.

Current interpretation:

Maturing consensus states appear behaviorally distinct from newly established consensus states and may represent vulnerable crowd configurations.

------

## Persistence Interpretation

Although the ontology is expressed as maturity states, those states are ultimately derived from persistence duration.

Reactive-JPY therefore implies:

Persistence Duration
↓
Consensus Maturity
↓
Crowd Failure Risk

Persistence is not explicitly modeled as the primary variable, but it is embedded within the ontology structure.

------

# Reactive-CHF Findings

## Original Hypothesis

Reactive-CHF began from a fundamentally different hypothesis.

Rather than consensus maturity, the CHF studies investigated whether volatility context influences crowd persistence and crowd outcomes.

The proposed ontology was:

Volatility
↓
Persistence
↓
Outcomes

------

## Volatility Findings

Strong evidence was found that volatility influences persistence duration.

Across both EURCHF and USDCHF:

- low-volatility environments support substantially longer persistence,
- high-volatility environments suppress persistence,
- ultra-long episodes occur almost exclusively in the lowest-volatility environments.

This relationship survives multiple analytical approaches.

------

## Outcome Findings

No meaningful evidence was found that volatility directly predicts crowd-failure probability.

However, a persistence-duration effect emerged.

Episodes lasting approximately 3–5 bars exhibit substantially lower crowd-failure rates than very short 1–2 bar episodes.

Observed difference:

-12.8 percentage points

The effect appears independently in both CHF pairs.

------

## Persistence Interpretation

Reactive-CHF therefore suggests:

Volatility
↓
Persistence Duration
↓
Outcome Probability

Persistence acts as an intermediary mechanism linking environmental conditions to behavioral outcomes.

------

# Comparative Analysis

Reactive-JPY and Reactive-CHF appear superficially different.

Reactive-JPY emphasizes:

- consensus maturity,
- lifecycle progression,
- crowd-failure vulnerability.

Reactive-CHF emphasizes:

- volatility environments,
- persistence duration,
- persistence-conditioned outcomes.

However, both frameworks ultimately revolve around persistence.

Reactive-JPY:

Persistence
↓
Maturity
↓
Failure

Reactive-CHF:

Volatility
↓
Persistence
↓
Failure

The two studies therefore may be describing different projections of the same underlying process.

------

# Candidate Latent Variable

A possible synthesis is that persistence represents a latent behavioral variable that is not directly observed but manifests through multiple observable mechanisms.

Possible manifestations include:

- sentiment persistence,
- consensus maturity,
- trend persistence,
- state survival probability,
- persistence-conditioned outcomes.

Under this interpretation:

- volatility influences persistence,
- persistence influences outcomes,
- maturity states are persistence-derived abstractions.

This would explain why persistence-related variables repeatedly emerge across otherwise independent research programs.

------

# Implications for BSVE

If persistence is genuinely latent, current ontology families may be incomplete descriptions rather than competing explanations.

Instead of:

Reactive
↓
Outcomes

or:

Volatility
↓
Outcomes

the underlying structure may be:

Environment
↓
Persistence
↓
Outcomes

Different ontology families may simply observe different portions of this chain.

This possibility remains speculative but is now supported by multiple independent findings.

------

# Open Questions

The following questions remain unresolved:

1. Is persistence the primary latent variable within BSVE, or merely one important mediator among several?
2. Do Persistent-family pairs exhibit analogous persistence-conditioned outcome behavior?
3. Are consensus maturity and persistence duration fundamentally different concepts, or alternative representations of the same phenomenon?
4. Does the historical Persistent-family partition remain optimal when evaluated through a persistence-centric framework?
5. Can persistence improve MPML regime selection more effectively than current ontology labels?

------

# Recommended Next Step

A Persistent-Family Re-Audit should be conducted before further ontology expansion.

The objective is not to validate the existing LV/HV/TF/MR framework.

The objective is to determine whether persistence-centered analysis produces a superior explanation of Persistent-family behavior.

Particular attention should be given to:

- volatility-conditioned persistence,
- persistence-conditioned outcomes,
- duration-dependent behavioral transitions,
- similarities and differences relative to Reactive-JPY and Reactive-CHF.

This study will determine whether persistence is a family-specific phenomenon or a broader behavioral principle capable of unifying multiple BSVE ontologies.
