# Latent Structure

This document summarizes the current interpretation of "latent structure"
within the MSML research framework.

---

# What Is Meant by "Latent Structure"?

Within the project, "latent structure" refers to:

> persistent conditional organization that is not directly observable
> from raw price movement alone.

Examples may include:

- persistence/release cycles
- volatility-conditioned behavior
- crowd synchronization
- structural asymmetry
- transition geometry
- adaptive positioning behavior

The hypothesis is NOT that markets are fully deterministic.

Instead, the current interpretation is that:

> markets may temporarily organize into partially persistent
> behavioral configurations.

---

# Why Latent Structure Matters

If markets contain latent structure, then:

- some behaviors should recur conditionally
- transfer learning should partially work
- behavioral families should emerge
- architecture robustness should increase
- downstream adaptive systems should react coherently

Several current findings increasingly support this interpretation.

---

# Evidence Suggesting Latent Structure

Current evidence includes:

- persistent/reactive family differentiation
- cross-family transfer asymmetry
- structure surviving sentiment ablation
- robustness across MLP/LSTM architectures
- partial downstream MPML persistence
- ABM/DL behavioral similarity

No individual result is decisive.

However, the combined pattern increasingly appears non-random.

---

# Structural vs Sentiment Components

Recent ablation experiments suggest that latent structure may contain
multiple partially overlapping layers.

---

## Structural Layer

Appears related to:

- volatility
- trend organization
- liquidity adaptation
- endogenous persistence

This layer survives:
- sentiment removal
- architecture changes
- downstream integration

---

## Sentiment Layer

Appears related to:

- local asymmetry
- release dynamics
- instability
- reactive transitions
- routing sensitivity

This layer appears:
- sparser
- more conditional
- more locally disruptive

---

# Pair-Family Structure

One of the strongest latent-structure findings is the emergence of
pair-family differentiation.

Persistent families appear structurally smoother and more stable.

Reactive families appear more release-driven and unstable.

This increasingly suggests that:

> latent structure may not be globally uniform across FX markets.

---

# Current Interpretation

The project increasingly interprets latent structure as:

> conditional behavioral geometry emerging from adaptive market participation.

This interpretation remains speculative,
but increasingly consistent across:
- DL,
- ABM,
- and downstream adaptive-system behavior.

---

# Open Questions

Major unresolved questions include:

- Is latent structure causal or emergent?
- How stable are latent manifolds?
- Are structures volatility-conditioned?
- Are families fundamentally different?
- Does structure survive regime transitions?
- How much structure is sentiment-specific?
- How much is purely endogenous?

These questions remain active research directions.
